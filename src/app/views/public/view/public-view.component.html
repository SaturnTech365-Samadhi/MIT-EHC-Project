Elephants and Honey Bees


We explores the use of Beehive Fences as a natural elephant deterrent, helping protect rural communities and farmlands. The idea is based on our innovative research using elephantsâ€™ fear of honeybees to help reduce crop-damage and minimize other human-elephant conflict incidences. Honey Bees are effective way to reduce damage from crop-raiding elephants and social and economic boost to poverty-stricken rural communities through the sustainable harvesting of Elephant-Friendly Honey.




Real-time Elephant Detection & Monitoring


We use Cameras installed in zone areas to obtain live feed of the area. If elephants approach, our system is able to distinguish elephants and send notifications to the main system. Additionally, our system is capable to capture unexpected incidents via cameras and alert the main system to take required actions.




Incident Reporting and Management


Authorized farmers can view live feed of their zones and report unexpected situations through our system.This is a effective proactive method to prevent unexpected disastes early.

Community Awareness


Spreading knowledge about elephants is important in minimizing human-elephant conflict and support human-elephant coexistence. In doing so, we are increasing conservation awareness both amongst those that live with elephants and around the world. All articles, stories and publications freely available in our website, which is a huge resource for elephant enthusiasts all over the world.
